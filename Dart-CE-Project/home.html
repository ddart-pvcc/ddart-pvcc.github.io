The budget of the United States government is an annual 150-page document that details the funding allocations to the various departments within the government.
This website provides a historical recap of the accomplishments of the federal government in 2021 - 2022 and descriptions of some of the 2023 funding distributions.
The highlights of 2021-2022 were:

  • 1.4 trillion dollars cut in federal deficit.
  • 6.5 million new jobs were created.
  • 5.7 percent economy growth.
  • Unemployment fell to 3.7 percent.
  • American Rescue Plan Act of 2021 was passed.
  • Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act was proposed and now passed.
  • Rejoined the Paris Agreement.
  • Federal Sustainability Plan launched.
  • Hate Crimes Law.
  • Rejoined World Health Organization.
  • Reinforced United Nations Human Rights Council.

Analysis of the United States Budget

The budget of 2023 is reflective of Executive Orders that were proposed and implemented in 2021 - 2022. The major Executive Orders (EO) that are now in place are:

  • EO 13985: Advancing Racial Equity and Support for Underserved Communities.
  • EO 13988: Preventing and Combatting Discrimination on the Basis of Gender Identity or Sexual Orientation.
  • EO 14005: Ensuring the Future is Made in All of America by All of American Workers.
  • EO 14017: America’s Supply Chains.
  • EO 14020: Establishment of the White House Gender Policy Council.
  • EO 14035: Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, and Accessibility in the Federal Workforce.
  • EO 14036: Promoting Competition in the American Economy.
